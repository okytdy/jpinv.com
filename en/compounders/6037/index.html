01 · REGIME

Price Action · 24 Months

Rakumachi sells monthly listing subscriptions to Japanese real-estate brokerages that want to put income-producing properties — rental apartments, parking lots, small commercial buildings — in front of individual investors looking to buy them. It earns that audience for free by ranking organically in Japanese-language searches for income-producing real estate, and by running a YouTube channel of teaching videos for first-time landlords that crossed one billion cumulative views in late 2025. The stock's September 2025 peak followed five quarters of operating-margin expansion from 45% to 55%, before the May 2026 restatement of the company's headline page-view series re-opened the question of how fast that audience is actually growing.

01 · THE RALLY Rakumachi runs one product: the rakumachi.co.jp portal. As of January 2026 it has about 487,000 registered individual investors using the site for free, and 5,459 brokerage agencies paying a monthly fee to list their income-producing properties on it. The business has three revenue lines on top of that. A patented proposal feature pushes off-market listings to investors who have told the site what they are looking for, a Premium subscription for individual investors who want extra tools (ARPU grew about 17% YoY in H1 FY7/26), and tie-up video ads sold to property managers and financial institutions. The OPM is high for three reasons. First, the company spends almost nothing on customer acquisition: free members find the site through twenty years of Japanese-search ranking and a YouTube channel that passed one billion cumulative views in late 2025. Second, the Premium subscription and the tie-up video product give the company pricing room. Third, a separate ¥4.08bn US-dollar bond portfolio (held to maturity, 5.5% average coupon) earns about ¥114M every six months in interest income, which lands below the operating line. The stock rose from ¥492 on May 16, 2024 to ¥1,493 on September 16, 2025 — about a triple. Three disclosures drove that move. The FY7/24 annual results in September 2024 showed revenue up 13% to ¥2.36bn with OPM at 44.9%, while the company moved ¥4.17bn of cash into the new US-dollar bond portfolio. The FY7/25 annual results on September 12, 2025 showed revenue up 33.6% to ¥3.16bn and OP up 45.6% to ¥1.55bn, with OPM at 48.9%. On the same day, the company announced a CEO succession from founder Naohiro Sakaguchi to Ryo Fujie (a long-tenure marketing executive), plus a fourth buyback in twelve months of up to 500,000 shares and ¥750M.

02 · THE CONSOLIDATION From the September 2025 peak the stock fell by about a third over the next eight months, reaching ¥990 on March 13, 2026. The business itself kept doing well during this period. At the October 2025 annual shareholders' meeting, the board expanded from four directors to seven, adding two long-tenure department heads as inside members. The founder kept his 68.2% holding; what changed was that day-to-day operations now had more people between him and the next decision. The Q1 FY7/26 results in December 2025 and the H1 FY7/26 results in March 2026 each showed OP up 43.4% YoY on revenue up 14.9%. H1 OPM came in at 55.3% on cumulative sales of ¥1,757M. So the pullback was not about the company slowing down. The simpler reading is that the September 2025 peak — which more than tripled the May 2024 low of ¥492 — was reached on thin trading volume. Sakaguchi family ownership is 68.2%, the only other disclosed 5%+ holder is VIS Advisors at 5.08%, and the effective free float that institutional buyers can actually trade is about a quarter of the share count. A thin float lets a small flow of buying push the price a long way up, and it also lets a small slowing of that buying push the price most of the way back down.

03 · WHERE WE STAND NOW On May 25, 2026 the company filed two announcements at the same time. The first was a new buyback: up to 600,000 shares (3.09% of shares outstanding ex-treasury) for up to ¥500M, running through November 27, 2026. The second was a correction notice. The company restated the page-view figures it had reported in six prior filings, going back to the FY7/25 annual results. Importantly, the correction was only about page-view counts. No revenue or profit number was changed. The reason is simple: brokerages pay Rakumachi a flat monthly fee for each listing they post — they do not pay per page-view. So restating page-views does not change anything the company actually bills. The error itself grew with each later release. The FY7/25 annual page-view total was restated by −0.6%, the Q1 FY7/26 figure by −5.6%, and the H1 FY7/26 figure by −8.5%. After the correction, H1 FY7/26 YoY page-view growth falls from +23.2% to +12.7%. The company described the cause as an internal review of its page-view aggregation process and stopped there. The next four quarters will answer three things: whether the corrected page-view series gets a methodology note and a multi-year backfill so audience growth is auditable; whether the buyback pace deepens to match the ¥4.08bn US-dollar bond portfolio (about two-thirds of total assets, earning a fifth of what the same yen earns inside the operating business); and whether the niche moat is widening or maturing as Japanese peers trade at 6.7x forward EV/EBIT against Rakumachi's 10.0x.

02 · CONTENTION

Live Investor Debates

Three open questions surface in the company's recent quarterly disclosures and in the May 25, 2026 correction notice. Each one settles a different part of the multiple.

DEBATE 01 · DISCLOSURE INTEGRITY
Is the page-view correction a single process fix that closes with one methodology footnote, or a disclosure-quality signal that re-rates the operating-engine narrative downward?
BULL Bulls observe that revenue and profit numbers were not restated, that agency contracts are per-listing rather than per-impression so the page-view figure is descriptive rather than load-bearing for cash flow, and that the corrected half-year operating margin still expanded eleven points year-on-year to 55.3%. The corrected page-view total still grew +22.8% in FY7/2025 and +12.7% in the first half of FY7/2026, leaving the directional trajectory intact. The view holds if the Q3 FY7/2026 release in June 2026 publishes a three-year restated monthly page-view series with a methodology footnote, and the rate of new error correction in subsequent quarters falls to zero.
BEAR Bears observe that the error magnitude grew with each successive disclosure — 0.6% in the FY7/2025 annual, 5.6% in the Q1 FY7/2026 tanshin, 8.5% in the half-year figure — a pattern more consistent with structural drift in the aggregation pipeline than with a one-time bug. The notice was packaged the same day as a new buyback authorization, the company has named no external assurance provider, and the eight-year audit relationship with Ark LLC has not produced a comment letter on the corrected series. The view holds if the FY7/2026 annual disclosure in September 2026 introduces no third-party KPI assurance and contains a further downward restatement of any prior-period operating metric.
DEBATE 02 · CAPITAL DEPLOYMENT
Is the ¥4.08bn US-dollar bond portfolio (about two-thirds of total assets) a deliberately maintained foreign-currency reserve that disciplines the operating business, or excess capital earning a fifth of the operating return the same yen would earn redeployed in the platform?
BULL Bulls observe that the company has authorized 850,000 shares and ¥800M of buyback in FY7/2026 to date (the third and fourth tranches across the trailing twelve months), that share count has dropped from 21.15M to 19.75M issued through the October 2025 cancellation of 1.4M treasury shares, and that the FY7/2026 dividend-per-share guidance of ¥13 marks a fifth consecutive year of progressive lift. The held-to-maturity dollar-bond portfolio earns 5.5% versus Japanese deposit rates, and the family's 68.2% position aligns the founder with continued buyback compression rather than scrip-funded growth. The view holds if the FY7/2027 capital-return cadence reaches at least ¥1.5bn cumulative buyback plus dividend with the bond-portfolio carrying value falling below ¥3.5bn during the year.
BEAR Bears observe that roughly two-thirds of total assets sit in non-operating securities, that the stated dividend payout target remains 20% while the operating business earns a return on capital of 28.2%, and that twenty years without a single acquisition or overseas expansion produces a value-extractive rather than value-creative deployment record. The portfolio's FX exposure was disclosed as a material risk factor in the FY7/2025 annual securities report, and management has published no multi-year total-return framework anchored to a portfolio drawdown timetable. The view holds if the FY7/2026 annual disclosure in September 2026 reaffirms the 20% payout target without introducing a stepped floor, and the bond-portfolio carrying value ends FY7/2026 above ¥4bn.
DEBATE 03 · MOAT TRAJECTORY
Is the specialist niche-portal moat widening as the partner-store base laps a 24-times-untapped agency total available market, or maturing as the corrected page-view growth rate halves and member-acquisition decelerates?
BULL Bulls observe that the partner-store count rose to 5,459 at the half-year mark against an addressable base of about 130,000 licensed brokerages in Japan, that YouTube subscriber count grew +35.3% year-on-year to 1.45M with cumulative views passing one billion in late 2025, and that the Premium retail-investor subscription added +14.7% subscribers with average revenue per user up roughly seventeen percent. The portal still has no scaled domestic competitor in the specialist investment-property category. The view holds if the Q4 FY7/2026 release in September 2026 prints partner-store count above 5,700, registered-member growth above +13% year-on-year, and Premium-subscriber growth above +15%.
BEAR Bears observe that registered-member growth has slowed from +18% in FY7/2022 to +13.2% in FY7/2025 to +12.4% in the first half of FY7/2026, that the corrected half-year page-view growth has halved from +23.2% to +12.7%, and that partner-store growth at +9.0% is the slowest pace in the disclosure window. The FY7/2025 annual securities report flags entry by the generalist mass-market portals as the explicit competitive threat. The view holds if the FY7/2026 annual disclosure prints partner-store growth below +9%, registered-member growth below +12%, and corrected page-view growth below +12% year-on-year.

The correction notice closes the past tense but leaves the forward tense open. Publishing the supplementary disclosure — a three-year restated monthly page-view series with the methodology footnote describing the corrected aggregation logic, a limited-assurance opinion from a third party (the existing auditor Ark LLC, or a SOC-style provider) on the rebuilt pipeline, and a quarterly cadence for the methodology footnote going forward — converts the May correction from an open question into the audited baseline the listed peer set publishes by default. The portal economics survive a slower page-view print; what does not survive is twelve more months of the market discounting the entire operating engine because the headline indicator has not been audited. Costs roughly ¥3–5M per year in audit fees plus an internal IT compliance pass.

The total payout already runs five times the stated 20% dividend-payout target, but the framework that anchors the cadence is still single-tranche, quarter-by-quarter buyback notices. Replacing the policy — a stepped payout-ratio target of 30–40% with a separate total-return floor of 80–100% of net income, plus a published three-year bond-portfolio drawdown timetable that releases roughly ¥1.0–1.5bn per year from non-operating securities into capital return — converts an over-capitalized balance sheet from an annual observation into a multi-year re-rating event. The yen redeployed against an operating-business return of 28% earns roughly five times what it earns sitting in the dollar bond portfolio. Costs management one board resolution and one disclosure footnote.

The Sakaguchi family bloc at 68.2% caps how much institutional money can enter the name and is the structural reason a 48.9% operating margin trades at single-digit forward EV/EBIT. Pairing a secondary offering — a controlled sale of roughly three to four million shares from founder holdings into the institutional channel, lifting effective free float above the TSE Prime threshold of about 35% by tradable shares — with a published Prime application timetable converts a fixed governance discount into a glide-path. The application package itself forces committee-set compensation, English-language IR, and a maturation of disclosure that the listed peer set already delivers. Costs the founder personal taxes on the sale; corporate cost is the legal package for the upgrade. Earliest disclosure window is the October 2026 annual general meeting.
