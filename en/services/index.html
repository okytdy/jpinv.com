Services — IR translation, interpretation, diagnostic retainers

English-disclosure mandate. Multi-vendor inconsistency. We hear this every week.

Earnings summaries, AGM notices, integrated reports, timely disclosures, mid-term plans — typically commissioned vendor-by-vendor, with terminology drifting across documents and across years. JII centrally manages your term base and translation memory, and co-develops the AI-assisted production tooling (terminology guides, automated check scripts) with your team. What's different about JII

One brain for terminology, memory, and AI tooling.

Most IR translation vendors quote by document. JII maintains, for each client, a single canonical term base, a single translation memory, and a set of AI-driven production tools (terminology guides and automated check scripts) co-developed with your team. On request, those production assets can be delivered to you as part of the engagement scope, so a real piece of automation infrastructure stays with your IR team.

01

Centrally managed term base

Company names, position titles, segment labels, KPI terminology, internal expressions. One term base per client, maintained by JII. Renderings stop drifting between documents.

02

Centrally managed translation memory

Past Japanese-English segment pairs accumulate into a translation memory that flows across earnings summaries, integrated reports, and AGM notices — consistency across years happens by default, not by manual review.

03

AI-assisted production tools

Your document formats, workflow conventions, and terminology policy are encoded into terminology guides and automated check scripts. We build them with your IR team, not in isolation.

04

Automation assets, available to you

The terminology guides and scripts we co-develop can be delivered to you on request as part of the engagement, so they remain yours to re-use, extend, or hand to another vendor later. No black box.

05

Pricing materially below direct-to-IR vendors

Central term base, translation memory, and AI tooling compress the per-document labor required for from-scratch translation. We pass that through as quotes materially below the rates direct-to-IR vendors typically charge. See the pricing page for the structure.

06

Confidentiality and access logs

Per-engagement NDA. For pre-disclosure information, we log who accessed which document and when. Aligned with financial-translation industry practice for insider-information handling.

Track B — The retainer's central deliverable

The Foreign IR Diagnostic Card

The recurring deliverable that sits at the center of the Light / Standard / Pro retainer. A 12-item, 24-point review of how a Japanese listed company's IR actually reads and sounds to foreign capital — one document, written for the IR / CFO / corporate-planning desk, updated every quarter or every year on retainer.

See retainer pricingDiscuss the diagnostic

The card tells you, item by item, where foreign investors stop, what they ask twice, and what they walk away unsure about.

It is not a stylistic critique of your English. It is the structural review that runs alongside translation work — and across roadshows, activist responses, and disclosure cycles — to keep the investor narrative coherent over time.

  • 12-item, 24-point scorecard
  • Top 3 communication gaps
  • Specific evidence from your IR materials and observed meetings
  • Recommended next fix — what to change first
  • Built for IR / CFO office / Corporate Planning

Track A vs. Track B

What each track is good for

Both are first-class JII offerings. The natural flow is to enter through a one-off and convert to a retainer if it lands, but one-off-only engagements are equally welcome.

Track A — Standalone answers

"Translate this document, accurately and consistently — now."

Earnings summaries, integrated reports, timely disclosures, interpretation engagements — done document-by-document and meeting-by-meeting. Because everything runs through JII's centrally managed TB / TM and AI tooling, the cross-vendor inconsistency problem doesn't follow you in. Engage us for a single piece of work.

Track B — Recurring answers

"Maintain English IR quality across documents and across years."

Quarterly or annual cadence. Capital allocation, competitive moat, growth premise — whether the investor walks out of the room with the investment case, year after year. The Diagnostic Card surfaces what to fix; the retainer maintains the fix across disclosure cycles.

Track B — what the retainer delivers

Recurring retainer deliverables

On retainer (Light / Standard / Pro), these are the deliverables that update every quarter or every year. For one-off engagements, individual deliverables can be scoped on their own — ask us.

01

The Diagnostic Card

12 items, 24 points, a single shareable document for IR / CFO / corporate-planning use.

02

Top 3 communication gaps

Where investor understanding is breaking down most — narrowed to three, not a long list.

03

Evidence and examples

Quotes from your IR materials and observed meetings showing exactly where the gap is happening.

04

Recommended fixes

06

Optional implementation path

If you want to act on the findings — translation, review, interpretation, meeting prep — scoped against the diagnostic.
