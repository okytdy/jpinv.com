Investor 1-on-1 Interpretation

Investor 1-on-1 interpretation that doesn't kill the pace of the conversation.

Putting an English speaker in the room is not the same as IR-grade interpretation. In a short 1-on-1 you cannot afford to lose investor intent, slow the Q&A, or restate guidance imprecisely. We support 1-on-1, small-group, and online interpretation for investor meetings — connecting numbers, outlook, capital policy, and business explanations without forcing pauses.

When clients ask us about this

We confirm format, participants, and likely topics in advance, and identify the points most likely to stall on the day.

We pace the responses for consecutive meetings and align terminology across overlapping themes.

On sensitive topics we cross-check phrasing against prior disclosures and the results presentation in advance.

We agree roles in advance assuming audio lag and overlapping speech, so re-asks don't break tempo.
